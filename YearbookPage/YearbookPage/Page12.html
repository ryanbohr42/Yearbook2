GOING UP IN SMOKE

Two stage crew members describe how the smoke machine
made the antics backstage more dramatic than the show itself

DYLAN PASHKE ('16)

"it was the senior citizen show, and everything
was going well. we were sitting backstage and
all of a sudden we saw smoke coming from the
back passageway. it takes a while for the smoke
machine to warm up, so we had to pre-heat it
during intermission in order for it to be ready

by the last scene. However, it actually turned on
during intermission and we did not realize until
it was too late. The smoke was spreading, some
might even say it was billowing. The smoke started
rising from under the stage into the scene
before it was needed!"

EMILY GAGGIOLI ('16)

"Pushing a freshman out of the way, I dove
under the stage trying to find the off-button
through all the fog. I did eventually find the
button, but the whole backstage area was full of
smoke. Still in a bit of a panic, I had everyone
running around trying to disperse it. As I passed

a crack in the set, I saw that the stage was
completely fogged over. There was only three
minutes before the next scene started, so there
was nothing I could do. Thankfully the cloud
started to lift, but the actors may or may not
have gone on stage with a bit of smoke."
